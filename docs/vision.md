# Vision

A 30-second storytelling experience where the user *is* in the story.

## The pitch

User opens the page. One button: **Begin**. They grant camera access, and for 30 seconds a narrator tells them a short story while Decart's realtime style-transfer model repaints what the webcam sees — frame by frame, beat by beat — to match what the narrator is describing.

It's not "watch a story". It's "be inside one".

## Why this is interesting

- **Realtime restyle** turns the user's own face/room/light into the canvas. The story isn't pre-rendered — it's about *them*, right now.
- **Short** (~30s) means it's shareable, repeatable, and the prompt-swap pacing is tight enough to feel intentional rather than glitchy.
- **Audio-first narration** (ElevenLabs, coming next) gives the experience a spine. Visuals follow the voice.

## Non-goals (for the prototype)

- Not a try-on tool. Not a filter app. Not a long-form video player.
- Not multi-user. Not interactive (yet — user doesn't choose paths).
- Not pixel-perfect. The charm is in the loose, painterly transformation, not photoreal accuracy.

## Success looks like

A person clicks Begin, watches their reflection become a forest, a cavern, a sunrise — hears a voice tell them a story for half a minute — and at the end says "wait, what?" and clicks again.
